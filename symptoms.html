Down Syndrome

SYMPTOMS

Physical & Cognitive Traits

  • Flattened face
  • Short stature
  • Upward-slanting eyelids
  • Poor muscle tone - very flexible
  • Cognitive impairment - poor short & long term memory
  • Protruding tongue
  • Small head - baby-like facial features

Life Expectancy

The current life expectancy of individuals with Down Syndrome is currently 60 years old (Diagnosis & Treatment, n.d.). This is a tremendous improvement over the life expectency in the past, which we talk about in the FUTURE section.

Fact or Fiction: Is Down Syndrome a Progressive Disorder?

No. There are no “stages” to Down Syndrome as the child will have symptoms from birth. However, people with Down Syndrome have a higher risk of developing Alzeimers and other memory-impairment diseases, which will progress over time (What Is Down Syndrome?, 2021).
