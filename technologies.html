Down Syndrome

2 TECHNOLOGIES FOR DOWN SYNDROME

Monitoring - Echocardiogram:

50% of babies with Down Syndrome are born with CHD:

CHD - Congenital Heart Disease; High blood pressure in the lungs, blue-tinted skin from limited oxygen in the bloodstream, and the heart cannot efficiently pump blood (What Conditions, 2021). For those born with CHD, echocardiograms are often used to monitor their hearts (Echocardiogram, n.d.).

PROCEDURE: (Echocardiogram, n.d.)

  1. Transducer - A wand-shaped device encoded in gel - The transducer is spread onto the patients chest.
  2. The transducer sends high-frequency sound waves (ultrasound) to the heart.
  3. The heart echoes the high-frequency sound waves, which are then recorded by the transducer.
  4. The transducer records the echoes from the heart, and a computer is used to convert the sounds into moving images.

Note that an echocardiogram makes it very easy to track blood flow, and if the patient has CHD, the blood flow would be blocked or significantly slower in the monitored valve. This is an obvious indicator, which is why an echocardiogram can be used to monitor the patient's symptoms, if they have Down Syndrome and need to be closely monitored. (Echocardiogram, n.d.).

Diagnosis - Karyotypes:

Karyotyping involves drawing a blood sample from the baby to examine their blood cells, photographing the chromosomes to detect trisomy 21. This can either be done prenatally, or after birth to confirm a diagnosis (What is Down Syndrome?, 2021).
